Suits (TV Series)

Suits is an American legal drama television series created and written by Aaron Korsh. The show premiered on June 23, 2011, on the cable network USA, and concluded on September 25, 2019, after nine seasons and 134 episodes.

Overview

The series revolves around Harvey Specter (Gabriel Macht), a senior partner at a top law firm in Manhattan, and his recently hired associate attorney Mike Ross (Patrick J. Adams). What sets Mike apart is his eidetic memory and his genuine law expertise despite never attending law school. The show focuses on Harvey and Mike managing to close cases while maintaining Mike's secret.

Themes and Setting

Suits is set at a fictional law firm in New York City, and later in the series, the firm undergoes several name changes due to shifts in leadership and mergers. The show features a slick, polished aesthetic and fast-paced dialogues. It delves into various themes such as mentorship, the cost of success, and the moral ambiguity of high-stakes law practice.

Reception

The show has been well-received for its sharp wit, characters, and performances. It also gained recognition for its stylish wardrobe, particularly the impeccable suits worn by the characters, contributing to the show's name and allure.

In [2]:
url = 'https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Suits_episodes'

response = requests.get(url)

soup = BeautifulSoup(response.text, 'html.parser')

tables = soup.findAll('table', {'class': 'wikitable plainrowheaders wikiepisodetable'})

viewership_data = []

season = 0
for table in tables[:-1]:
    rows = table.find_all('tr')
    for row in rows[1:]:
        cols = row.find_all('td')
        if cols:
            episode = cols[0].text.strip()
            viewers = cols[-1].text.strip()
            
            viewers = float(viewers[:4])
            episode = int(''.join(filter(str.isdigit, episode)))
            
            if episode == 1:
                season += 1
            
            viewership_data.append((season, episode, viewers))

df = pd.DataFrame(viewership_data, columns=['Season', 'Episode', 'Viewers'])

General Comment¶

Viewership Over Time

The first graph depicts a comprehensive trajectory of 'Suits' viewership across all nine seasons on an episode-by-episode basis. It unveils that the series premiered with robust ratings, garnering approximately 4.64 million viewers for the first episode. This high level of initial engagement indicates a strong audience interest at the outset of the series.

As the show progressed, a decline in viewership became evident, demonstrating a typical pattern for long-running TV series where audience numbers often diminish over time. Notably, the drop from the premiere episode of the first season to the finale of the last season underscores this trend, with the viewership dwindling to just 0.86 million.

Average Viewership Per Season

The bar chart showcasing average viewership per season further substantiates this downward trend. Season 1 enjoyed the highest average viewership, indicating that the show had a compelling start. Thereafter, each subsequent season experienced a decrease in average viewership, with Season 9 registering the lowest, reflecting a gradual viewer attrition as the series neared its conclusion.

Episode-to-Episode Viewership Change

The episodic viewership change graph reveals the volatility in viewership between individual episodes. While some episodes experienced a surge in viewers, others saw a decline. This fluctuation might be attributed to several factors, including episode story quality, cliffhangers, and seasonal events affecting television viewing habits.
